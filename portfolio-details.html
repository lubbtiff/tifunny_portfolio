Pain Point

  • Productivity pain points result in customers wasting their time and achieving less than they ideally would. A product might have unnecessary complexity or require users to complete too many steps, resulting in dissatisfaction.
  • Support pain points arise when clients don't receive the help they need at different stages of the customer experience. They may not have easy access to sales representatives
  • Financial pain points occur when customers feel they are not getting the value their money deserves. A company's product might be more expensive than its competitors' offering, require costly maintenance or have a confusing pricing structure. Consumers avoid purchasing from companies that make it difficult to determine the final cost of a product with hidden fees or changing rates.
